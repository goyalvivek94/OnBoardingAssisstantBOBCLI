# Institutional AML Risk Assessment Matrix

## Core Risk Categories
[cite_start]The institution uses a multi-tiered matrix to assign an initial risk rating (Low, Medium, High) to clients during onboarding based on four core pillars[cite: 10]:

### 1. Geographic Risk
* [cite_start]**High Risk**: Jurisdictions subject to sanctions, high corruption indexes, or identified by FATF[cite: 10, 11].
* **Low Risk**: FATF-compliant member states with robust domestic regulatory regimes.

### 2. Product and Service Risk
* **High Risk**: Private banking, trade finance, anonymous prepaid cards, or accounts involving complex cross-border wire transfers.

### 3. Delivery Channel Risk
* [cite_start]**High Risk**: Non-face-to-face onboarding, digital-only identification, or relationships initiated via independent third-party intermediaries[cite: 10].
